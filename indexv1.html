Realish Diwali

Realish Diwali is an ongoing research project that reimagines traditional Diwali celebrations through a multiplayer virtual reality experience. Focused on sustainability and cultural preservation, the experience blends magical realism with environmental messaging to simulate firecracker bursting — minus the pollution. This project also explores the adoption of digital rituals and aims to understand how immersive storytelling and gamification can drive behavioral change. The project is designed both as a pedagogical tool and a research study at the intersection of XR, culture, and sustainability.

The Silence that Remains

The Silence that Remains is an immersive XR art concept in progress, being developed for submission to India HCI. The project was sparked by the evocative composition "The Silence that Remains – Mokshamu Galada" by Indian Carnatic Progressive Rock band Agam, and draws artistic inspiration from the revolutionary volumism style pioneered by VR artist Anna Zhilyaeva. At its core, the piece reflects on themes of climate change, loss, and inner stillness. More will be shared once the work is ready for public showcase.
